Literature Dissertation

My dissertation, titled "Immersive Metaphors of Reading In Narrative Theory," investigates the pervasive metaphors of immersion in reading experiences. I argue that while the immersive understanding of narrative is often viewed as intuitive, many metaphors surrounding it are contradictory and fundamentally alter how we discuss narrative experiences.

The work is divided into several key chapters:

  • Chapter 1: Immersion into Fictional Worlds
  • Chapter 2: Immersion as Mind-Reading
  • Chapter 3: Immersion as a Fantasy Performance
Each chapter unravels different aspects of narrative immersion, challenging mainstream definitions and exploring their implications on reader experience and critical reception.

This analysis draws on significant literary theories and aims to uncover the invisible assumptions that shape our understanding of narrative and its immersive qualities. You can view the full dissertation here.
